Project: How important is education?

My project aims to investigates the importance of education and the impacts it has on society. This project looks at different levels of education, and its effects on income, employment, happiness and democracy.

Firstly, It must be addressed that levels of education differ significantly around the world. I created three maps to show the disparities in attendance/completion of primary, secondary and tertiary education globally. The darker areas of each map show a higher proportion of the population that have either attended or are attending the different levels of education.

Impact on earnings:

Clearly, education is not equal around the world, and this could be a reason why quality of life and so many other factors are also unequal. A huge reason for inequality is difference in income, so I decided to look at education's impact on that and see how much of the inequality education can explain. Firstly, insteas of diving straight into salaries, I wanted to see how education effects the likelihood of getting a job in the first place.

This chart shows the clear increase in average income as an individual obtains a bachelor's degree. Impact on wellbeing:

Not only does education have an impact on the money side of life, but it also has strong links with happiness and quality of life. I decided to look at three indicators when thinking about wellbeing. Firstly, I know that citizens in democratic countries tend to have a better way of life, since there is more freedom and people are more in control. I thought there must be a relationship between education and democracy, since in general when people learn about political systems and study history, democracy stands out as a superior system.

Source 1 Source 2 Colab

This regression shows the change in democratic scores and compulsory years of education over the past 20 or so years, we can see that as compulsory years of schooling have risen around the world, so has the democratic scores. In particular as years progress, the relationship has got stronger, and the R2 score has nearly doubled. Therefore, stricter education laws and further encouragement of schooling may improve the quality of life in dictatorship countries.
